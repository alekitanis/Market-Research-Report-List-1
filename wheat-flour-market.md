<p><p>The wheat flour market comprises various types tailored for specific culinary applications. Self-raising flour includes baking powder, suitable for cakes and quick breads. Bread flour has a high protein content, ideal for yeast doughs. Biscuit flour features lower protein for tender biscuits, while cake flour is finely milled for light, airy cakes. Wholemeal flour retains bran and germ for added nutrition. Resultant flour is a by-product of other milling processes, and semolina, coarser and used for pasta, offers distinct texture and flavor.</p></p>
