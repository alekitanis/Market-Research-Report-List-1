<p><p>The artificial tissue heart valve market can be segmented into transcatheter and surgical heart valves. Transcatheter heart valves are designed for minimally invasive procedures, allowing placement via catheterization, which reduces recovery time and hospital stays. Surgical heart valves, on the other hand, require open-heart surgery for implantation, often used in more complex cases. Both types aim to restore proper heart function, addressing issues like stenosis or regurgitation, but cater to different patient needs and clinical scenarios.</p></p>
