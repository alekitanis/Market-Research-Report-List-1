<p><p>The Medical 3D Bioprinting market encompasses several printing technologies, including Stereolithography (SLA), Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM), and Laminated Object Manufacturing (LOM), among others. SLA utilizes ultraviolet light to cure liquid photopolymer into solid structures, enabling high-resolution prints. FDM uses melted thermoplastic filaments, suitable for creating durable models. LOM combines layers of material, like paper, which are bonded and cut to form 3D objects. Other methods may include inkjet bioprinting and extrusion-based techniques, catering to various biomedical applications.</p></p>
